Keep LA Housed Plan

The Keep LA Housed plan is designed to provide a transparent approach to rent increases, ensuring that tenants are protected from excessive hikes while allowing for fair adjustments based on 60% of inflation, with a maximum annual increase set at 3%. Our proposal would apply to all rental units.

Current RSO

The Current Rent Stabilization Ordinance allows for rent increases to be at 100% of the CPI (Consumer Price Index). The Current RSO also sets a minimum of 3% and a maximum of 8% rent increase annually, plus one percent for gas and/or one percent for electric added on each year. Single-family homes, condos, and buildings constructed after 1978 are exempt from these limits.

LAHD Proposal

The Los Angeles Housing Department's proposal allows for a minimum of 2% and a maximum of 5% increase annually, with the option to bank unused increases for future years. This means tenants end up paying more even when inflation goes down year-to-year. Why is LAHD protecting landlords from inflation?

About the Keep LA Housed Plan

  • The Keep Los Angeles Housed Plan was created by the Keep LA Housed Coalition, DSA-LA, and regular tenants just like you!
  • The Keep Los Angeles Housed Coalition plan aims to provide a fair and transparent approach to rent increases in Los Angeles, ensuring that tenants are protected from excessive hikes while allowing for some reasonable adjustments based on inflation.
  • The plan would apply to all rental units, including single-family homes and condominiums, which are currently exempt under the existing Rent Stabilization Ordinance (RSO).
  • The plan proposes to limit rent increases to 60% of the Consumer Price Index (CPI), with a maximum annual increase set at 3%. This means that if inflation is low, rent increases will be minimal, and if inflation is high, rent increases will still be capped at a reasonable level.
  • The Keep LA Housed plan also includes provisions to prevent landlords from evicting tenants in order to raise rents, as well as measures to ensure that tenants are informed about their rights and have access to resources for legal assistance.
  • The Keep LA Housed Coalition is made up of a diverse group of organizations and individuals who are committed to fighting for affordable housing and tenant rights in Los Angeles.
  • We believe that everyone deserves a safe and stable place to call home, and we are working to make that a reality for all Angelenos.
  • We believe that the time has come for Los Angeles City Council to adopt a rent control policy that puts tenants first and ensures that everyone has access to affordable housing.
